'Playing very well with rich musical expression.'

- Ian Brown

24-year-old Hong Kong Pianist Yeeching Lau is active as a soloist, accompanist, chamber musician and repetiteur. She was invited by British Isle Music Festival as a soloist and chamber pianist in 2023, and she is the winner of the Edward Brooks Lieder Accompanist Prize in 2024.

Yeeching performs both solo and chamber music extensively. Her performance engagements included concert at Royal Birmingham Conservatoire, St Stephen Walbrook, Shenstone Library in UK; Guangzhou Mufeng Concert Hall and Dalian Urban Music Centre in China.

In addition to the recent competitions and concert, she has also got rewards over the years, including the winner of the Delia Hall Accompanist Prize for Pianist in 2022, the First Prize in the Sixth Star & Torch Art Talent Competitions of Chinese Youngsters Northeast Area in 2019, Asian Cities Classical Music Competition hosted by the Japanese Classical Music Association, the First Prize of The 6th Asia Music Competition (Northeast Area), and the second prize of The 81st Steinway International Teenagers Piano Competition in 2017, and the Excellent Award in Northeast Area Piano Professional Youth Group of the 2016.

Yeeching's musical pursuits extend beyond solo piano performances. She is deeply passionate about chamber music, collaborating with a diverse range of musicians, including string, wind, and brass band performers from around the world. Her dedication to her craft is evident in her participation in masterclasses with esteemed musicians such as Ian Brown, Wei-Wei Li, Jianing Kong, Yunjie Chen, and chamber masterclasses with Jian Wang, principal members of London Symphony Orchestra and Castalian Quartet.

As repetiteur, She has worked on Royal Birmingham Conservatoire 2023 Opera Scenes as repetiteur. In the February 2024, she worked as repetiteur on Royal Birmingham Conservatoire Spring Opera: Cinderella.

After studying with Professor Xiaoqiang Wang since she was five, Yeeching studies for her bachelor's degree at the Royal Birmingham Conservatoire with Dr. Robert Markham, followed by her master's degree under Dr. Robert Markham and Daniel Browell. With a master's degree with Distinction from RBC, Yeeching continues to push the boundaries of her musical career. She is currently pursuing an Advanced Postgraduate Diploma in Opera Repetiteur at RBC with Paul Wingfield and Robin Bowman, supported by scholarship.
